    2. Evolution of Practice: Student vs. Professional

    How does "demonstrating competency" look different in a classroom versus an urban field setting?

    Competency Area Classroom Expectation (Student) Field Expectation (Urban Context)
    Comp 1: Self-Regulation Managing reactions to difficult readings or peer feedback without becoming defensive. Maintaining composure when an urban client is in crisis or expressing anger at systemic failures.
    Comp 1: Electronic Communication Writing professional emails to professors; respectful discussion board posts. Documentation in medical records; Maintaining boundaries on social media (NASW 1.07).
    Comp 6: Empathy & Engagement Role-playing active listening; identifying bias in self-reflection papers. Building trust with a family who has historical mistrust of systems (police/DCFS) due to trauma.
    Comp 8: Inter-professional Collaboration Group projects; respecting diverse learning styles of peers. Collaborating with lawyers, teachers, and doctors to coordinate care in a fragmented urban system.
    Comp 10: Trauma Lens Understanding ACEs (Adverse Childhood Experiences) theory. Recognizing that a client's "resistance" may be a trauma response to the urban environment, not defiance.

    3. Simulation: The eviction Notice

    Test your ability to integrate these competencies in a high-pressure Urban scenario.

    Scenario Setup

    You are an intern at an agency in a high-density urban housing complex. You are visiting the Martinez family. The father, Mr. Martinez, meets you at the door. He is visibly agitated, pacing, and yelling about an eviction notice he just received. He yells, "You social workers are all the same! You just want to take my kids!"

    This situation triggers Competency 6 (Engagement) and Competency 10 (Trauma Lens).

    How do you respond?

    Outcome


    Once the situation de-escalates, Mr. Martinez shows you the eviction notice. It is legally complex. You are a social work student, not a lawyer.

    This triggers Competency 8 (Intervene/Collaborate) and Competency 1 (Professional boundaries/scope).

    What is your intervention?
