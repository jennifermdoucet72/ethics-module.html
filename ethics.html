The Ethical Review Board Simulation

Interactive Module for Social Work Ethics (NASW Code)

Instructions

Role: You are members of the State Ethical Review Board.

Task: Review the assigned complaint/consultation. You must issue a formal ruling citing specific NASW Ethical Standards.

Process:

  1. Review your assigned scenario below.
  2. Complete the "Ethical Matrix" (Identifying conflicts, codes, and values).
  3. Wait for the instructor to reveal the "Wildcard Twist."
  4. Adjust your ruling based on the new information.

The Scenarios

Scenario A: The "Small Town" Struggle

Premise: You are a social worker in a rural community (only provider for 30 miles). Your new client, in crisis over a divorce, is your daughter's soccer coach. If you refer him out, he likely won't receive care due to distance.

Key Codes to Consider: 1.06 (Conflict of Interest), 6.04 (Access to Care).

Scenario B: The Colleague's Secret

Premise: You notice your mentor, Sarah, often smells of alcohol after lunch. She asks you not to tell the supervisor, claiming she is going through a divorce and "has it under control."

Key Codes to Consider: 2.09 (Impairment), 2.08 (Taking Action).

‚ö†Ô∏è THE TWIST: You discover Sarah made a charting error yesterday that resulted in a patient receiving the wrong discharge instructions (potential harm).

Impact: Has the threshold moved from "Consultation" to "Duty to Report"?

Scenario C: The Digital Dilemma

Premise: A former client sends a friend request with a message: "I'm doing great! Look at my new baby." You are tempted to accept to validate their progress.

Key Codes to Consider: 1.06 (Conflicts), 1.07 (Privacy).

‚ö†Ô∏è THE TWIST: You check the client's public profile and see a post threatening self-harm, posted 10 minutes ago.

Impact: You "crossed a boundary" to see the post, but now have life-saving information. What is your duty now?

Debriefing Points

  • Scenario A (Rural): Before the twist, we manage the dual relationship. After the twist (anger at parents), objectivity is compromised. 1.06(c) requires action to minimize conflict; referral is likely mandatory now.
  • Scenario B (Impairment): Before the twist, we talk to the colleague (2.09). After the twist (patient harm), we must prioritize client safety over colleague loyalty. Reporting to administration is now required.
  • Scenario C (Digital): Before the twist, accepting is a boundary violation. After the twist, 1.07(c) applies: Privacy can be breached to prevent "serious, foreseeable, and imminent harm." Call 911/Welfare check.

Top Cited Standards

Code Name "Real Talk" Translation
1.01 Commitment to Clients Client First. Loyalty is to the client (unless they will hurt someone).
1.02 Self-Determination Their Choice. They can make bad decisions if they aren't dangerous.

1.07 Privacy & Confidentiality The Vault. Exceptions: Danger to self/others, abuse, court order.
