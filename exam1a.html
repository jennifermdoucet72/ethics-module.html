Part 2: Case Study Applications

Case Study 1: The Rural Coach (Dual Relationships)

A social worker in a small, rural town provides outpatient therapy to Mr. Miller, a father dealing with recent unemployment and depression. The social worker's daughter plays on the local youth soccer team, which is coached by Mr. Miller. The social worker regularly attends practices and games, creating an unavoidable non-professional relationship.

10. Analyze the primary ethical conflict present in this scenario. Outline the **three most important steps** the social worker should take to manage this unavoidable dual relationship while maintaining professional responsibility.

11. Consider the "Appearance of Conflict" (Standard 1.06). If the social worker attends the soccer tournament and Mr. Miller perceives her cheering for her daughter as "taking sides" against his coaching decisions, how does this damage the therapeutic alliance? How should the social worker proactively address this specific boundary crossing with the client?

12. If the social worker determines that her objectivity is too compromised to treat Mr. Miller effectively, but there are no other providers available (a common rural challenge), how does she balance the ethical requirement to avoid conflicts of interest with the ethical mandate to prevent "Abandonment" (Standard 1.16)?

Case Study 2: The Threat (Duty to Warn)

David, a long-term client with a history of anger management issues, arrives at a session highly agitated. He states, "My neighbor, Sarah, is the reason I got fired, and I am going to make her pay. I know where she works, and I am going to wait for her after she clocks out tomorrow and hurt her badly so she never bothers anyone again."

13. Determine the social worker's immediate primary ethical and legal obligation. Justify your answer by referencing the specific components of the client's statement (Specific, Serious, Imminent) that trigger the duty to act.

14. Standard 1.07(c) states that when breaking confidentiality, social workers should disclose "the least amount of confidential information necessary." In this scenario, distinguish between what information you would share with the police versus what information you would share with Sarah (the potential victim).

15. Assume the immediate crisis is managed (the police intervene). David returns to the clinic the following week. How should the social worker address the breach of trust with the client? Discuss the ethical considerations in deciding whether to continue treating David or to refer him to another provider.
