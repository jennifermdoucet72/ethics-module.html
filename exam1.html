Case Study 1: The Rural Coach (Dual Relationships)

Scenario: You are a clinical social worker in a very small, rural community. You are one of only two mental health providers in the county; the other provider is currently not accepting new patients.

A new client, Mr. Miller, comes to you for help with severe anxiety related to a pending divorce. During the intake assessment, Mr. Miller mentions that he is the head coach of the local girls' soccer team. You realize immediately that your 14-year-old daughter is on that specific team. Mr. Miller does not yet know that you are the parent of one of his players.

Mr. Miller becomes emotional during the session, disclosing that he often feels "angry and resentful" toward the parents of the players, feeling they judge him too harshly. He specifically mentions that he is worried about "keeping his cool" at the upcoming tournament this weekend. You are planning to attend that tournament to watch your daughter.

Relevant Competencies: Competency 1 (Ethical Behavior) & NASW Standard 1.06 (Conflicts of Interest).

Part 2: Question Bank (By Exam)

Exam 1: Core Values & Principles

Q1: Service Points: 5 | Difficulty: 2

According to the NASW Code of Ethics, the primary goal of social workers is to help people in need and to address social problems. Explain how a social worker distinguishes between their personal desire to 'do good' and the professional obligation of 'Service' as defined in the ethical principles. Give one example of a behavior that fits the professional definition.

Q2: Social Justice Points: 5 | Difficulty: 2

Define 'Social Justice' within the context of the NASW Code of Ethics. Briefly discuss one specific way a social worker might challenge social injustice when working with a population oppressed by poverty or unemployment.

Q3: Dignity and Worth Points: 5 | Difficulty: 3

The value of 'Dignity and Worth of the Person' requires social workers to treat clients with care and respect. Describe a scenario where a client's behavior challenges a social worker's personal values, and explain how the worker maintains this ethical standard despite the conflict.

Q4: Integrity Points: 5 | Difficulty: 2

Ethical Principle: Social workers behave in a trustworthy manner. Explain the concept of 'Integrity' regarding the accurate representation of one's skills. Why is it an ethical violation to claim expertise in a modality (e.g., EMDR or CBT) for which you have not been supervised or certified?

Q5: Competence Points: 5 | Difficulty: 3

Standard 1.04 (Competence) mandates that social workers provide services only within the boundaries of their education. If a social worker is asked to treat a client with a condition they have never encountered, what are the specific ethical steps they must take to continue treating that client?

Exam 2: Client Rights & Boundaries

Q1: Self-Determination Points: 5 | Difficulty: 2

Standard 1.02 (Self-Determination) states social workers respect a client's right to define their own goals. Identify and explain the specific exception to this rule where a social worker is ethically obligated to limit a client's self-determination.

Q2: Informed Consent Points: 5 | Difficulty: 2

List three specific elements that must be included in a valid 'Informed Consent' discussion (Standard 1.03). Why is it insufficient to simply have the client sign a form without verbal explanation?

Q3: Dual Relationships Points: 5 | Difficulty: 3

Define a 'Dual Relationship' (Conflict of Interest Standard 1.06). Explain the difference between a boundary crossing and a boundary violation, and provide an example of a prohibited dual relationship in a clinical setting.

Q4: Privacy and Tarasoff Points: 5 | Difficulty: 4

Regarding Standard 1.07 (Privacy and Confidentiality): Explain the 'Duty to Warn/Protect' (Tarasoff). What specific criteria must be met for a social worker to break confidentiality without a client's consent to protect a third party?

Q5: Sexual Relationships Points: 5 | Difficulty: 2

Standard 1.09 prohibits sexual activities with current clients. Explain the NASW Code of Ethics' stance on sexual relationships with specific relatives or friends of the client, and the stance on relationships with former clients.

Exam 3: Professional Relationships & Practice

Q1: Unethical Colleagues Points: 5 | Difficulty: 3

You witness a colleague making derogatory comments about a client in the breakroom. According to Standard 2.01 (Respect) and 2.10 (Incompetence/Unethical Conduct), what is your first ethical obligation before reporting them to administration?

Q2: Impairment Points: 5 | Difficulty: 3

Standard 2.09 addresses Impairment of Colleagues. If you suspect a colleague is impaired by substance use and it is interfering with their practice, but no clients have been harmed yet, what is the required course of action?

Q3: Interdisciplinary Collaboration Points: 5 | Difficulty: 4

When working on an interdisciplinary team (Standard 2.03), a doctor proposes a medical intervention that violates social work values of client self-determination. How should the social worker ethically handle this disagreement within the team setting?

Q4: Referral for Services Points: 5 | Difficulty: 2

Standard 2.06 (Referral for Services) requires referring clients when other professionals have specialized knowledge the social worker lacks. Explain why 'holding on' to a client for financial reasons or emotional attachment is an ethical violation.

Q5: Supervision Points: 5 | Difficulty: 3

Standard 3.01 (Supervision and Consultation) states supervisors should not supervise family members or close friends. Explain why this constitutes a Conflict of Interest and how it impacts the concept of 'Vicarious Liability.'

Exam 4: Cultural Competence & Records

Q1: Cultural Competence Points: 5 | Difficulty: 3

Standard 1.05 (Cultural Awareness and Social Diversity) was updated to include 'Cultural Humility.' Explain the difference between simply 'knowing about' a culture and practicing 'Cultural Humility' in the assessment phase.

Q2: Termination Points: 5 | Difficulty: 3

Standard 1.16 (Termination of Services) prohibits client abandonment. Describe the difference between 'Abandonment' and 'Termination.' What steps must a social worker take if they leave an agency to ensure they are not abandoning their clients?

Q3: Access to Records Points: 5 | Difficulty: 4

Standard 1.08 (Access to Records) generally grants clients access to their files. Under what specific, 'compelling' circumstance can a social worker limit a client's access to their own records?

Q4: Documentation Points: 5 | Difficulty: 2

Standard 3.04 (Client Records) requires documentation to be timely and accurate. Ethically, why is documenting a session three weeks after it occurred considered a risk to the client, even if the social worker has a 'good memory'?

Q5: Bartering Points: 5 | Difficulty: 4

Standard 1.13 (Payment for Services) addresses bartering. While bartering is generally discouraged, the Code allows it under very specific, limited circumstances. Name two conditions that must be met for bartering to be ethical.

Exam 5: Technology & Private Conduct

Q1: Social Media Points: 5 | Difficulty: 2

Standard 1.06 (Conflicts of Interest) and the technology standards advise against accepting friend requests from clients. Explain how accepting a friend request creates a 'Dual Relationship' and risks breaching client confidentiality.

Q2: Electronic Search Points: 5 | Difficulty: 3

Is it ethical for a social worker to Google a client or check their public court records without the client's consent? Reference the standard regarding privacy and respect in your answer.

Q3: Telehealth Points: 5 | Difficulty: 2

When providing services remotely (Standard 1.07 Privacy), what specific conversation must the social worker have with the client regarding the client's physical environment during the session?

Q4: Private Conduct Points: 5 | Difficulty: 3

Standard 4.03 (Private Conduct) states social workers should not permit their private conduct to interfere with their professional responsibilities. Discuss how a social worker's political rant on a public social media profile could be interpreted as a violation of this standard.

Q5: Solicitation Points: 5 | Difficulty: 2

Standard 4.07 (Solicitations) prohibits testimonial solicitation from vulnerable clients. Why is it unethical to ask a current therapy client to write a 5-star Google Review for your private practice?
